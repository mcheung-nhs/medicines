Co-codamol is a mixture of 2 different painkillers – paracetamol and codeine.

It’s used to treat short-lasting aches and pains including headaches, muscular pain, migraine and toothache. It can also be used to reduce fever (38C and above).

It’s usually taken when everyday painkillers, such as aspirin, ibuprofen and paracetamol alone, haven't worked.

Co-codamol comes in 3 different strengths. You can buy the lowest-strength co-codamol from pharmacies. The higher strengths are only available on prescription.

This medicine comes as tablets and capsules.

Co-codamol can be given to children from the age of 12. For over-16s, read our information on co-codamol for adults.

  1. Key facts

    • The most common side effects your child might get are constipation, feeling sick and sleepiness.
    • Giving your child too much co-codamol can be harmful. Don’t be tempted to increase the dose or give a double dose if their pain is very bad.
    • It’s possible to become addicted to the codeine in co-codamol, but this is rare if your child is taking it to relieve pain under medical supervision.
    • Always leave at least 6 hours between doses and give a maximum dose of 4 co-codamol tablets in 24 hours.
    • Co-codamol is also called by the brand names Panadeine, Paracodol, Codipar, Medocodene, Zapain, Kapake, Solpadol and Tylex.

  2. Who can take co-codamol

    Co-codamol can be taken by children aged 12 and older. For children aged 16 and over, read our information on co-codamol for adults.

    Do not give co-codamol to children under the age of 12 as it can cause severe breathing problems.

    Co-codamol is not suitable for some children. Tell your doctor before starting the medicine if your child:

    • has lung problems or breathing difficulties
    • has a head injury
    • has adrenal gland problems
    • has an illness which causes fits
    • has liver problems
    • has had their tonsils or adenoids removed because of a sleep problem called obstructive sleep apnoea syndrome.

  3. How and when to give

    Co-codamol comes as tablets and capsules. They are swallowed whole with a drink of water.

    Give your child co-codamol either before or after food.

    If they find it difficult to swallow tablets or capsules, co-codamol is also available as soluble tablets that dissolve in water to make a drink.

    How much to give

    Co-codamol comes in 3 different strengths.

    The amount of codeine it contains can be 8mg, 15mg or 30mg.

    All 3 strengths contains 500mg of paracetamol - the same as in a standard paracetamol tablet or capsule.

    The strength of co-codamol appears as 2 numbers on the packet. For example, the strength may be written as 8/500. This means it contains 8mg of codeine and 500mg of paracetamol.

    You can buy the lowest strength of co-codamol (8/500) from a pharmacy without a prescription. The higher strengths (15/500 and 30/500) are only available on prescription from a doctor.

    How often to give it

    The normal dose for children aged 12 to 15 is 1 tablet of co-codamol up to 4 times a day.

    Always leave at least 6 hours between doses.

    The total maximum dose is 4 co-codamol tablets in 24 hours.

    Overdosing on co-codamol can cause serious side effects. Don’t be tempted to increase the dose or give your child a double dose if their pain is very bad.

    Do not give your child co-codamol that you have bought for more than 3 days. If the pain doesn’t improve after 3 days, talk to your doctor.

    The total maximum dose of co-codamol for children aged 12 to 15 is 4 tablets in 24 hours. Wait at least 6 hours between doses.

    What if I forget to give it?

    If you forget a dose of co-codamol, don’t worry, just give it to your child when they next need pain relief. Then continue as before, giving doses every 6 hours if needed.

    Never give your child 2 doses at the same time. Never give an extra dose to make up for a forgotten one.

    What if they take too much?

    Giving them 1 or 2 extra tablets of co-codamol by accident is unlikely to be harmful. If this happens, wait at least 24 hours before giving your child any more co-codamol.

    Giving more than 1 or 2 extra tablets can cause serious side effects. If your child has taken an accidental overdose they may feel very sleepy, sick or dizzy. They may also find it difficult to breathe. In serious cases they can become unconscious and may need emergency treatment in hospital.

    If your child feels sleepy, sick, dizzy or finds it difficult to breathe, call 111 for advice straight away.

    If your child needs to go to hospital, take the box or leaflet inside the packet plus any remaining medicine with you.

  4. Giving them co-codamol with other painkillers

    It’s safe to give children co-codamol with other types of painkiller that don’t contain paracetamol such as ibuprofen.

    Never give aspirin to a child under the age of 16 (unless prescribed by a doctor).

    Don't give them co-codamol with paracetamol, or other medicines that contain paracetamol, because of the risk of overdose. This includes some painkillers (tramacet and co-dydramol), migraine remedies and cough and cold remedies (Lemsip and Night Nurse).

    Before giving them any other medicines, check the label to see whether they contain paracetamol.

  5. Side effects in children

    Like all medicines, co-codamol can cause side effects although not everyone gets them. Most children have no side effects or only minor ones.

    Your child is more likely to have side effects if they take the higher strengths of co-codamol.

    Common side effects

    Common side effects happen in more than 1 in every 100 children. Tell your doctor if they bother your child or don’t go away. Common side effects include:

    • constipation
    • feeling sick or vomiting
    • feeling sleepy
    • headache

    Serious side effects

    Serious side effects are rare and happen in less than 1 in 1,000 children.

    Tell a doctor straight away if your child has:

    • a skin rash
    • difficulty peeing
    • heart problems
    • changes in their eyesight
    • dizziness

    Serious allergic reaction

    In rare cases, it's possible to have a serious allergic reaction to co-codamol.

    A serious allergic reaction is an emergency. Contact a doctor straight away if you think your child is having a serious allergic reaction.

    The warning signs of a serious allergic reaction are:

    • getting a skin rash that may include itchy, red, swollen, blistered or peeling skin
    • wheezing
    • tightness in the chest or throat
    • having trouble breathing or talking
    • swelling of the mouth, face, lips, tongue or throat

    These aren't all the side effects of co-codamol. For a full list, see the leaflet inside your medicines packet.

  6. How to cope with side effects

    What to do about:

    • constipation – encourage your child to eat more high-fibre foods such as fresh fruit and vegetables and cereals. They should try to drink several glasses of water or another liquid each day. If they can, it may also help to do some gentle exercise.
    • feeling sick or vomiting – Give co-codamol with or just after a meal or snack. Feelings of sickness should normally wear off after a few days. Talk to your doctor about giving your child an anti-sickness medicine if it carries on for longer.

    You can report any suspected side effect to a UK safety scheme.

  7. Cautions with other medicines

    Some medicines interfere with the way co-codamol works. And co-codamol can interfere with the way some medicines work. Tell your doctor if your child is taking:

    • epilepsy medicines
    • medicines to stop them feeling sick or vomiting such as domperidone or metoclopramide
    • medicines to treat infection, particularly rifampicin or ciprofloxacin
    • antidepressants – some types don't mix with co-codamol

    Mixing co-codamol with herbal remedies and supplements

    It's not possible to say that complementary medicines and herbal teas are safe to take with co-codamol. They're not tested in the same way as pharmacy and prescription medicines. They're generally not tested for the effect they have on other medicines.

    Tell your pharmacist or doctor if your child is taking any other medicines, including herbal remedies and supplements.

  8. Common questions

    How does co-codamol work?

    Co-codamol contains paracetamol and codeine. These 2 painkillers work in different ways to relieve pain.

    Paracetamol seems to work by blocking chemical messengers in the brain that tell us we have pain. Paracetamol also reduces fever by affecting the chemical messengers in an area of the brain that regulates body temperature.

    Codeine belongs to a group of medicines called opiates. It affects pain receptors in the central nervous system and the brain to block pain signals to the rest of the body. When codeine blocks the pain receptors, there are other unwanted effects - for example slow and shallow breathing. It can also slow down digestion, which is why codeine can cause constipation.

    When will your child feel better?

    Co-codamol takes up to an hour to work. It keeps on working for about 5 hours.

    If your child has taken co-codamol for 3 days and the pain has not gone away then speak to your doctor.

    Is co-codamol addictive?

    Yes, taking co-codamol regularly for a long time could make your child addicted to the codeine in it. But in reality, if they are taking co-codamol to relieve pain (rather than using it as a recreational drug) it's very unlikely they will get addicted to it because they are not taking it to get a high.

    If they need to take co-codamol for a long time their body can become tolerant to it. That means they need higher doses to control their pain. This isn't usually a problem when they stop taking co-codamol. Withdrawal symptoms can be prevented by reducing treatment gradually.

    Is it safe to take co-codamol for a long time?

    It's not usually recommended to take co-codamol for longer than 3 days.

    Taking co-codamol regularly for a long time can lead to addiction, which might make your child feel restless and irritable when they stop taking the tablets.

    Talk to your doctor if you're worried about addiction.

    Are there other painkillers they can try?

    Yes, there are other painkillers they can try.

    Some painkillers work better than others for certain pains. For example, the best painkiller to ease a headache may not be the best one for backache.

    Before giving your child co-codamol, try paracetamol to see if that helps the pain. Paracetamol can relieve most types of pain.

    Painkillers known as non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medicines (NSAIDs), such as ibuprofen, can reduce inflammation as well as pain. These are good for cold symptoms and aches and pains after an injury like a sprain. However, NSAIDs aren't suitable for some children. This includes children with stomach ulcers or severe heart, kidney or liver problems. If you would like to try NSAIDs please speak to your pharmacist or doctor.

    Can your child ride a bike with it?

    Co-codamol can make them feel sleepy or dizzy. If this happens, they shouldn't ride a bike until they feel better.

    Can co-codamol cause weight gain?

    There's no evidence that taking co-codamol will make them put on weight or lose it.

    Is there any food or drink they need to avoid?

    They can eat and drink normally while taking co-codamol.
